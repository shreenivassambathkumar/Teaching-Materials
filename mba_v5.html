Artificial Intelligence (AI) üéØ

üî≠ The Wide Field of View

**AI** is the **widest circle**, the entire scientific field of trying to make machines **mimic human intelligence**. This includes anything that makes a computer seem "smart," whether it uses simple rules or complex learning.

üî¨ Zooming In: Learning from Data

**Machine Learning** is a **tool or subset inside AI**. This specific method achieves intelligence by making the computer **learn from data** (getting better with experience), rather than being told every single step or rule.

Think of it as: **A machine learning rules** by looking at thousands of examples.

üîé Ultra-Zoom: The Neural Network

Rule-Based vs. Machine Learning: The Detailed Evolution

üìú
Rule-Based Systems: History & Limits ‚öôÔ∏è

üß© Defined by "If-Then" Logic

  • **Core Logic:** Systems use **if-then** or **if-else statements** for problem-solving.
  • **Historical Example:** The **General Problem Solver (1950)** by Herbert A. Simon & Allen Newell, designed to break problems into smaller ones.
  • **Early Application:** **MYCIN (1970s)**, which diagnosed bacterial infections, showcased AI's potential for **practical applications**.

**Limitations:**

  • **Lack of Flexibility:** Operates strictly within **predefined rules and boundaries**.
  • **Adaptability:** **Difficult to adapt** to new, uncoded situations or domains.
  • **Complexity/Stability:** Increased complexity leads to **stability challenges** and difficult maintenance.
üöÄ
Rise of Machine Learning (ML) üìà

üóìÔ∏è The 1990s Shift in AI Development

The need to overcome the limitations of rule-based systems drove the rise of **Machine Learning** in the **1990s**.

  • **Core Function:** Learns **patterns** and makes decisions based on the **data** it processes, not explicit programming.
  • **Adaptation:** Learning from data allows ML to **adapt and improve** its performance over time.
  • **Nature:** It is considered **Flexible & Powerful** due to its ability to handle complex and dynamic scenarios.
üí°
Key ML Learning Types (The 3 Varieties) üìö

üî¨ Varieties of Learning

These concepts define how an ML model acquires knowledge:

  • **Supervised Learning:** Learns from **labeled data** (input and correct output are provided).
  • **Unsupervised Learning:** Learns from **unlabeled data** to find hidden patterns or groupings.
  • **Reinforcement Learning:** Learns by **interacting with an environment**, receiving rewards for correct actions.
üîÆ
Analytics: Predictive & Prescriptive üß≠

üìà ML in Business Foresight

**Predictive Analytics (1990s)**

Uses **statistical techniques** to **forecast** trends and behavior. Used across finance, healthcare, and retail to **predict risk, demand, and customer preferences**.

**Prescriptive Analytics (Developed 2000s)**

Focuses on providing **recommendations** for optimal outcomes (e.g., "what should we do next?").

ML Breakthroughs in the 2000s: **Handwriting, Spam recognition, Speech Recognition.**
